How I Think About Growth at Vercel

Mental Model

Vercel’s growth engine sits at the intersection of discovery, product, and pricing, with self-serve at the core. Developers find Vercel via frameworks, content, SEO, and AI assistants; they onboard through a product-led experience; and they expand as their projects and teams grow.

As Head of Growth Marketing, I’d focus on:

  • Owning digital surfaces as growth assets — Vercel.com, key landing pages, docs entry points, SEO, GEO, and paid channels working as one system.
  • Aligning tightly with Growth Product around key activation milestones: first deploy, first project, first team invite, first production app.
  • Building a disciplined experiment loop where every test has a clear hypothesis, success metric, and rollout plan, and wins scale quickly.
North Star: Activated workspaces / projects Guardrails: CAC, payback, LTV / CAC Inputs: Traffic × Activation × ARPU

30 / 60 / 90-Day Plan

Execution

Days 0–30: Understand, Instrument, Prioritize

Goal: Get fluent in the funnel and choose the first 3–5 high-leverage experiments.

  • Map the growth funnel end-to-end: traffic sources → signup → first project / deploy → activation → upgrade → expansion.
  • Audit performance for SEO, GEO, paid, and on-site conversion (pricing, docs entry pages, templates).
  • Review experiments and analytics with Growth Product and Data; align on a clear north star for self-serve.
  • Segment users into practical cohorts (solo devs, agencies, startups, enterprise evaluators).
  • Stand up a simple, shared dashboard for funnel health and experiment impact.

Days 31–60: Ship High-Impact Experiments

Goal: Launch and learn from a focused set of tests across acquisition, activation, and monetization.

  • Acquisition: sharpen high-intent SEO and GEO, refine paid search/social around ICPs, and test new landers and offers.
  • Activation: experiment with onboarding flows, starter templates, and nudges to shorten time-to-first-deploy.
  • Monetization: test messaging and CTAs on pricing pages and in-app upgrade prompts aligned to usage thresholds.
  • Create a lightweight experiment doc format and a weekly growth standup to keep the loop tight.

Days 61–90: Scale What Works & Systematize Growth

Goal: Turn early wins into durable, repeatable programs.

  • Scale the best-performing campaigns and experiences while maintaining CAC / payback guardrails.
  • Build a rolling experimentation roadmap with Growth Product and Demand Gen so the engine keeps running after month 3.
  • Tighten reporting for leadership: a clear narrative around traffic, activation, conversion, and revenue.
  • Document learnings so future teammates build on a strong foundation rather than repeating tests.

Example Experiments I’d Run Early

Test & Learn

1. GEO & AI Discovery

  • Build structured “how to” content tailored for AI assistants (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini) around deploying apps, migrating from other hosts, and performance use cases.
  • Measure impact on qualified traffic, signups, and activated workspaces—not just impressions.

2. Framework-Specific Landers

  • Refine landing pages for key frameworks (Next.js, React, Svelte, etc.) with clearer ICP targeting and social proof.
  • A/B test messaging, CTAs, and templates to lift CTR and signup conversion.

3. Onboarding & Activation

  • Test first-run experiences and starter templates by cohort (solo dev, startup team, agency).
  • Combine in-app and lifecycle email nudges for users who sign up but don’t deploy, or deploy once and churn.

4. Upgrade & Expansion Nudges

  • Trigger contextual upgrade prompts at meaningful usage or team thresholds with crisp value messaging.
  • Experiment with small pricing / packaging tweaks and upsell surfaces while closely watching conversion and churn.
